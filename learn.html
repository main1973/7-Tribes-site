Crypto 101 — The Core Ideas

1) What is crypto?

Crypto is money on a public ledger. Instead of a bank keeping the records, a network keeps the records.

2) What is a blockchain?

A blockchain is a public database of transactions. Once recorded, it’s extremely hard to change. That’s the “trust layer.”

3) What makes a coin go up or down?
  • Supply + demand is the main driver.
  • Thin liquidity = price can move wildly.
  • Utility (real use), trust (transparency), and distribution (not whale-owned) matter long term.
4) What’s “gas”?

Gas is the small network fee to process transactions. Every chain has a gas token (ETH on Ethereum, $AKE on Alkebuleum).

Safety & Wallet Security

Seed Phrase Rules

  • Write it on paper. Store offline. Never screenshot.
  • Never share it. No admin, no support, nobody needs it.
  • Consider 2 copies in separate safe places.

Scam Radar

  • Random DMs offering “help.”
  • Fake airdrops / fake support forms.
  • Clone websites — always check the URL.

Common Myths

“Crypto is all scams.”

Scams are real. The defense is verification + transparency. If you can’t verify it, don’t touch it.

“If I lose my phone, I lose my crypto.”

You recover the wallet with your seed phrase. Phone lost ≠ coins lost.

“It’s anonymous crime money.”

Most chains are public ledgers. Transactions are traceable. Don’t confuse “addresses” with “invisible.”

“Crypto is illegal.”

Rules vary by country/state. This isn’t legal/tax advice — you’re responsible for local compliance.

Glossary (Plain Language)

Wallet — An app that holds your keys (your access). Protect the seed phrase.

Gas — Network fee to run transactions.

DEX / CEX — Places to trade (decentralized vs centralized exchange).

Liquidity — How easily something buys/sells without wild price changes.

Treasury — Community funds controlled by rules + voting.

DAO — Governance system: proposals + votes = action.
