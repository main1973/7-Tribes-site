Crypto 101 — Doubts & Answers

1) What makes a coin go up or down?

Short answer: supply + demand.

  • More buyers than sellers → price up.
  • More sellers than buyers → price down.

Also matters: utility (can you use it), trust (transparent devs/treasury), scarcity (fixed supply), and news/hype/fear.

2) What if a billionaire buys it all?

That’s a whale attack. Defenses we emphasize:

  • Wide distribution: more holders, less control per wallet.
  • Community treasury + voting: no one outsider dominates direction.
  • Liquidity pools: better absorption of big buys/sells.
  • Social defense: in an extreme abuse, communities can fork—whales know this.

3) What if they dump it cheap?

That’s price manipulation. Thin markets are fragile. We counter with:

  • Real usage: merchants + projects = baseline demand.
  • Lots of holders: dilution of whale power.
  • Radical transparency: live dashboard; receipts over rhetoric.
Street version: 1 guy owning 10 houses can play landlord. 10,000 families owning their homes? No single landlord can crash it. Same idea here.

Common Myths (Debunked)

“Crypto is all scams.”

Some is. 7TRB ties value to work you can verify: merchants, funded projects, and a public dashboard. If you can’t verify it, don’t trust it.

“If I lose my phone, I lose my crypto.”

Your wallet is recovered with your seed phrase. Store it offline. Phone lost ≠ coins lost.

“It’s anonymous crime money.”

Blockchains are public ledgers. Transactions are traceable. Good opsec matters, but it’s not a shadow realm.

“Crypto is illegal.”

Laws vary by country, but owning/using crypto is legal in most places. We don’t give legal/tax advice—know your local rules.

Safety & Wallet Security

Seed Phrase Rules

  • Write it on paper. Store offline. Never screenshot.
  • Never share it. No admin or “support” needs it. Ever.
  • Consider two copies in separate safe spots.

Device Hygiene

  • Update your OS & browser.
  • Use a password manager + 2FA for accounts.
  • Bookmark official links (this site, Etherscan contract page).

Scam Radar

  • Too-good-to-be-true “airdrops”.
  • DMs offering help. Fake support.
  • Clone websites (check URL carefully).

Best Practice

  • Small first test transactions.
  • Hardware wallet for larger holdings (Ledger/Trezor).
  • Verify contract: Etherscan.

Glossary (Plain Language)

Wallet

An app that holds your keys. It’s your account. Protect your seed phrase.

Gas

The small ETH fee to run a transaction on Ethereum.

DEX / CEX

Decentralized or centralized exchange where you trade coins.

Liquidity

How easy it is to buy/sell without moving price.

Treasury

Community funds controlled by proposals/votes.

DAO

Community governance: proposals + votes = action.
